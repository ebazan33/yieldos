SCHD vs VYM: Which dividend ETF actually wins in 2026?

Updated April 23, 2026 · Written by the YieldOS team

If you've spent more than ten minutes in any dividend investing subreddit, you've seen the same argument on loop: SCHD vs VYM. They're the two biggest, cheapest, most-held dividend ETFs in the US — and dividend investors rarely own just one, even though they absolutely should. This post cuts through the noise and gets to the real answer.

TL;DR: SCHD wins for dividend-growth investors who want higher yield and faster payout growth. VYM wins for investors who want maximum diversification and don't care about yield or growth as much as they care about "never having to think about it." In a 10-year horizon, SCHD has pulled ahead on both total return and income growth.

1. Yield: SCHD wins by ~90 basis points

SCHD yields about 3.82% versus VYM's 2.95% as of April 2026. That gap has been consistent — SCHD has out-yielded VYM by 60–110 bps for most of its lifespan. On a $100,000 position, that's roughly $870 more in annual dividend income. Not life-changing, but it compounds if you reinvest.

Why the gap? SCHD screens for a dividend-yield-weighted top-100, while VYM includes almost anything paying an above-average dividend. The wider net waters down the yield.

2. Dividend growth: SCHD wins decisively

This is the lever nobody talks about enough. SCHD's 5-year dividend CAGR is roughly 11% annually. VYM's is about 6%. That's not a small difference — it compounds aggressively:

3. Total return: SCHD has led, but not always

SCHD has outperformed VYM by roughly 2% annualized over the last decade on total return. That said, VYM has beaten SCHD in specific years — 2022 in particular, when energy names drove VYM's industrial-heavy basket higher. Over full market cycles, SCHD's quality screen (which excludes speculative yielders) has been a tailwind.

4. Holdings overlap: higher than most investors realize

About 50 names appear in both ETFs, which means roughly half of SCHD's portfolio is duplicated if you hold both. Top shared holdings in 2026: Home Depot, Verizon, PepsiCo, Pfizer, BlackRock, AbbVie. If you hold SCHD + VYM together, you're mostly getting SCHD's concentration with extra diversification noise from VYM's other 450 holdings.

If you want to pair SCHD with something genuinely different, the standard recommendations are DGRO (dividend growth focus, more tech exposure), DIVO (covered-call overlay for extra income), or an international dividend ETF like VYMI.

5. Sector weightings: the real difference

SCHD is heavier in Financials, Healthcare, and Consumer Staples, and notably light on Technology and Utilities. VYM is more balanced but heavier on Industrials. Neither holds meaningful tech — both dividend strategies filter out most of the FAANG basket (Apple pays, but Meta, Amazon, Alphabet, Tesla, Nvidia mostly don't qualify or weight heavily).

If you want dividend exposure plus tech, you'd need to add individual stocks (AVGO, MSFT, AAPL) or a tech-friendly ETF like DGRO.

6. Expense ratios: functionally identical

Both charge 0.06%. On $100K invested that's $60/year. This is not a decision factor.

Who should buy which?

Buy SCHD if…

  • You're a dividend-growth investor with a 10+ year horizon
  • You want higher current yield
  • You trust the quality screen to avoid value traps
  • You're OK with ~100-stock concentration

Buy VYM if…

  • You want maximum diversification (550+ stocks)
  • You prefer Vanguard's ecosystem
  • You care about total return over dividend growth
  • You're building a three-fund portfolio where VYM is the dividend tilt

The honest verdict

For most people reading this post — someone actively building a portfolio that pays them every month — SCHD is the better single choice. It yields more, grows dividends faster, has a quality screen, and costs the same. VYM is a perfectly fine second-tier option if you already own VTI/VOO and want a small dividend tilt in the Vanguard family.

The exception: if you're in a taxable account in a high tax bracket and need to minimize dividend income (because ordinary dividends push you up brackets), VYM's lower yield can be the right move. For everyone else, SCHD's 90-bp yield advantage and 5-point faster dividend growth is too much to give up.

Frequently asked questions

Should I own both SCHD and VYM?

Not usually. They overlap by about 50% of holdings, so owning both mostly dilutes SCHD's quality screen. Pair SCHD with DGRO or DIVO for meaningful diversification instead.

Is SCHD safe for retirement?

SCHD has one of the cleanest dividend-growth records of any high-yield ETF, and its underlying index applies quality screens (return on equity, cash-flow-to-debt) that screen out most "value traps." That said, no single ETF should be 100% of a retirement portfolio.

How often do SCHD and VYM pay dividends?

Both pay quarterly. SCHD's ex-dividend dates are usually in late March, June, September, and December. VYM's are similar.

Which has better tax treatment?

Both pay mostly qualified dividends, so both get the 0%/15%/20% qualified-dividend tax rate for US investors. VYM's slightly lower yield means lower tax drag in a taxable account, but the difference is small.
