Best Dividend Trackers in 2026: An Honest Comparison

Updated April 23, 2026 · Written by the YieldOS team

If you're a dividend investor, you've probably noticed that most "best dividend tracker" articles are thinly-veiled affiliate posts. This one isn't. We build a dividend tracker (YieldOS) — so we have opinions — but we'd rather help you pick the right tool than oversell ours. Here's an honest look at the six most popular options in 2026, what each does well, and who each is actually for.

We'll cover: Simply Safe Dividends, Snowball Analytics, Sharesight, Stock Analysis, Stock Events, and YieldOS. Skip to the quick verdict if you just want a recommendation.

What to look for in a dividend tracker

Before comparing products, it helps to know what you actually need. A good dividend tracker should do at least four things well:

  1. Accurate income projections. Your monthly and annual passive income should update automatically as prices and dividend rates change.
  2. Upcoming dividend calendar. You should see every ex-div and pay date across your portfolio in one place, not have to look them up stock by stock.
  3. Brokerage import. Typing in 47 holdings by hand is how trackers get abandoned in week two. CSV support is the baseline.
  4. Honest projections. No tool can predict the market, but the best ones use transparent assumptions (yield, dividend growth, contributions) you can tweak.

Nice-to-haves: safety grades, tax estimation, portfolio sharing, and a FIRE projection that tells you the exact year your dividends replace your salary. Some tools nail all of these; others skip them entirely.

The six trackers, ranked

1. Simply Safe Dividends

$499/yearBest for: safety-first investorsUS only

The grand old name in dividend safety analysis. Brian Bollinger's proprietary "Dividend Safety Score" is the industry reference — a 0–100 grade based on payout ratio, debt, earnings, dividend history, and more. If you're evaluating individual stocks for long-term holding, SSD is still the gold standard.

Pros
  • Best-in-class safety research
  • Detailed analyst commentary per stock
  • 20+ years of institutional credibility
Cons
  • $499/year — steepest on the list
  • Dashboard feels like 2012
  • No FIRE projection, no paycheck calendar
  • US stocks only (no TSX)

2. Snowball Analytics

Free + $7–14/moBest for: DRIP chart loversGlobal coverage

Snowball is beautifully designed and excellent at visualizing the "snowball effect" of reinvested dividends over time. The projection charts are genuinely delightful. If you're a visual thinker who wants to stare at a growth curve and dream about 2040, this is your tool.

Pros
  • Beautiful charts and UX
  • Generous free tier
  • Handles international stocks
Cons
  • Not income-first (defaults to total return)
  • Limited safety data
  • No US tax estimator

3. Sharesight

Free + $15–35/moBest for: tax-focused investorsGlobal coverage

Sharesight's tax reporting is unmatched — it's arguably the best tool in the world if you're in Australia, New Zealand, or the UK and need to file accurate capital gains and dividend income. For US investors, it's overkill: Sharesight's strength is being a portfolio tracker with dividend features bolted on, not a dividend-first app.

Pros
  • Institutional-grade tax reports
  • Handles 40+ stock exchanges
  • Strong for accountants
Cons
  • Complex — steep learning curve
  • Expensive at the tier that unlocks tax reports
  • Dividend features are secondary

4. Stock Analysis

Free + $10/moBest for: research, not trackingUS focus

Stock Analysis (stockanalysis.com) is fantastic as a research database — their per-ticker dividend history and financials pages are some of the cleanest on the web. As a tracker, though, it's minimal. You can enter a portfolio but there's no paycheck calendar, no FIRE projection, and limited alerting.

Pros
  • Clean per-stock research pages
  • Great free tier for screening
  • Fast, lightweight
Cons
  • Tracking is bolted on, not core
  • No income-first dashboard
  • No goal or FIRE tracking

5. Stock Events

Free + $6/moBest for: calendar-first mobile usersMobile only

Stock Events is a phone-first tracker built around an upcoming-dividends calendar. It's elegant, fast, and free-tier-friendly. The limitation is that it's really just a calendar — no FIRE projection, no AI insights, no goal tracking. If you want a beautiful mobile dividend calendar and nothing else, this is great.

Pros
  • Best-in-class dividend calendar UI
  • Excellent mobile app
  • Free tier is genuinely useful
Cons
  • Mobile-only (no real web app)
  • No FIRE planning
  • No tax estimator

Quick verdict: who should pick what

If you're a safety-first stock picker holding 20 individual dividend payers, Simply Safe Dividends is worth the $499. The research is that much deeper than everyone else.

If you're an Australian, UK, or NZ investor with complex tax reporting needs, Sharesight. Nobody comes close on tax.

If you want a free mobile calendar and not much else, Stock Events. Clean, focused, cheap.
