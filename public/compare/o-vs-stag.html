Diversification is the biggest difference

Realty Income owns 15,000+ properties across 85+ industries — 7-Eleven, Dollar General, Walmart, FedEx facilities, gaming resorts, data centers. Its top 10 tenants represent ~20% of rent. That's institutional-grade diversification.

STAG owns ~570 industrial warehouses, concentrated in the Midwest and Southeast. It's a pure-play bet on e-commerce logistics demand. Its top 10 tenants represent ~10% of rent — less concentration per tenant, but the entire portfolio is one sector.

The dividend track record gap

Realty Income has raised its dividend for 30 consecutive years and paid monthly for 55+ years. STAG has raised its dividend every year since 2013 — a 13-year streak. In a recession, O's track record matters more than STAG's slightly higher current yield.

Who should pick Realty Income

Pick O if you want a core dividend holding you can sleep with, if you're near retirement and can't afford a cut, or if you value the 30-year aristocrat status. It's in the S&P 500 and counts as a core REIT position.

Who should pick STAG Industrial

Pick STAG if you specifically want industrial/logistics real estate exposure, you believe e-commerce square footage demand keeps growing, or you want a smaller-cap REIT with more room to grow than O's $45B+ market cap.

Hold both?

Many dividend portfolios include both. A common allocation: O as a 5–10% core REIT position, STAG as a 2–3% sector tilt. That gives you monthly income from two different real estate segments without overconcentrating on any single REIT.

Frequently asked

Which has better total return history?

Realty Income has delivered slightly higher total returns over 10-year periods due to its combination of moderate dividend growth and multiple expansion. STAG has traded more volatility for comparable-to-slightly-lower total return. Past performance isn't indicative of future results.

Are either of these good for a Roth IRA?

Both are excellent Roth IRA holdings. REIT dividends are taxed as ordinary income in a taxable account, so sheltering them inside a Roth eliminates that tax drag entirely. Many FIRE investors hold O and STAG in their Roth specifically for this reason.

Do interest rates affect them equally?

Both are sensitive to interest rates — higher rates increase their cost of capital and compress property valuations. Industrial real estate (STAG) has historically been slightly more rate-resilient because of the e-commerce demand tailwind.

Is there a better monthly REIT than these two?

Other popular monthly REITs include AGNC (mortgage REIT, very high yield but higher risk), EPR Properties (experiential real estate), and Gladstone Commercial. None have the combined track record, scale, and quality of O. STAG remains the go-to pure-play industrial monthly payer.
