What makes YieldOS different

Most portfolio trackers start with "here's what your portfolio is worth today." We start with "here's when your next paycheck arrives and how much it is." That one framing difference cascades into every part of the product.

Paycheck calendar, not just charts

Every dividend appears on a real 12-month calendar. You see the stack: rent payments, utility bills, subscription drawdowns — alongside inbound dividend income. For retirees and near-retirees this is the only view that matters.

Path to FIRE projection, live

We project your exact year of financial independence using your current portfolio's yield, dividend growth rate, and your stated monthly contribution. Recalculates every time you add a holding.

Currency-aware from day one

Canadian (TSX) tickers pay in CAD. US tickers pay in USD. European tickers in EUR. We convert every dividend at the pay-date FX rate and show you a clean home-currency total — something Sharesight charges $25/month for and Snowball doesn't offer at all.

Free forever plan

The core experience is free, forever. The Seed tier supports up to 20 holdings — enough for the vast majority of dividend portfolios. Paid tiers exist only for power users who want unlimited holdings, CSV import, and the Daily AI Briefing.

Privacy-first

We don't sell your portfolio data. We don't show ads. We don't read-only link to your brokerage (no Plaid). You paste or import a CSV. We never ask for brokerage passwords, and we never will.

How we source data

Transparency matters for a finance tool. Here's where every number comes from:

  • Real-time prices & fundamentals — Financial Modeling Prep API (exchange-delayed quotes, licensed)
  • Dividend history & ex-dates — Financial Modeling Prep and cross-checked against each company's investor-relations feed
  • FX rates — Daily ECB reference rates, cached and refreshed every 24 hours
  • Estimated yield/CAGR figures — Computed by YieldOS from trailing-12-month distributions. Not broker-guaranteed.
  • Ticker pages & blog data — Educational estimates based on public historical records, clearly labeled as estimates. For live, current data use the app.

Any number you see on a public blog or ticker page on this site is an estimate derived from public historical data. The live numbers inside the app refresh on every load.

Security & infrastructure

YieldOS runs on Supabase (Postgres + authentication) hosted in AWS us-east-1, with rows encrypted at rest. Every account has Row-Level Security enforced — you can only read your own holdings, never another user's. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt; we never see them in plaintext.

We don't hold brokerage credentials. CSV imports are parsed client-side in your browser and only the structured rows (ticker, shares, cost basis) are sent to our servers. The underlying CSV never leaves your device unless you explicitly save a snapshot.

Who's behind this

YieldOS is a solo-founded indie project by a founder who got tired of paying $30/month for dividend tracking features that should be part of any decent broker. There is no VC behind us, no growth team, no paid influencers. Every feature you see was requested by users, debated in our Discord, and shipped by one person.

This matters because our incentives are aligned with yours. We don't need to sell your data to subsidize a free tier. We don't need to push you toward unnecessary upgrades to hit investor-mandated ARR targets. We need the product to be good enough that you pay $9–19/month voluntarily when you outgrow the free plan.

Our editorial approach

Every blog post, ticker page, and comparison guide on this site is written to be useful first and search-engine friendly second. We do not:

· Accept paid placements or sponsored content
· Link to affiliate broker sign-up pages
· Recommend specific securities
· Claim to provide investment advice

We do:

· Cite historical data with a clear "as of" date
· Label estimates as estimates
· Flag the tradeoffs of every dividend strategy (high yield = high risk)
· Update posts when the underlying data materially changes
