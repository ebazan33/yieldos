Why dividend investors own SCHD

SCHD is popular for three reasons:

  • Quality screen built into the index. The Dow Jones US Dividend 100 requires 10 consecutive years of dividend payments, plus composite screens on cash-flow-to-total-debt, ROE, and dividend yield. You get quality filtering for free.
  • Low expense ratio. 0.06% is cheap — roughly one-fifth the cost of actively managed dividend funds.
  • Reliable dividend growth. The ~11% 10-year CAGR on the underlying dividend per share compounds hard. $10k invested in 2015 would now yield considerably more than $10k at current prices of SCHD, purely from growth.

It's not a complete dividend portfolio — SCHD is underweight tech and healthcare relative to the S&P 500 — but it's a strong anchor holding. Most YieldOS users who hold SCHD pair it with 1-2 additional income ETFs (JEPI or DIVO) and a handful of individual dividend growers.

Frequently asked questions

When does SCHD pay dividends?

Quarterly — typically in the last week of March, June, September, and December. The ex-dividend date is usually a Wednesday or Thursday, with the pay date 7-10 business days later.

What is SCHD's current dividend yield?

SCHD's trailing 12-month yield has fluctuated between roughly 3.5% and 4.2% over the past two years. For the live number, check the SCHD card inside YieldOS or any brokerage quote page.

Is SCHD a good dividend ETF for retirement?

SCHD is commonly used as a core holding by dividend investors because of its quality screen, low fees, and dividend-growth track record. It's not a complete retirement portfolio on its own — most users pair it with broader equity exposure and bonds — but it's a reasonable anchor for the dividend-growth portion of a portfolio. This isn't investment advice; consult a licensed advisor for your specific situation.

Has SCHD ever cut its dividend?

SCHD's trailing 12-month dividend per share has increased every calendar year since inception in 2011. Individual quarterly payments fluctuate — Q2 and Q3 are typically smaller than Q4's larger "catch-up" — but on an annual basis, dividend growth has been consistent.

Is SCHD's dividend qualified for US tax purposes?

Substantially all of SCHD's distributions have historically been classified as qualified dividends, which are taxed at the preferential 0/15/20% rate in the US depending on your income bracket. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
